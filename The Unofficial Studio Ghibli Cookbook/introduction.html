The Magic of Studio Ghibli Cookbook

Studio Ghibli, the acclaimed animation film studio, was founded in 1985 in Tokyo by Hayao Miyazaki, Isao Takahata, and Toshio Suzuki. Known for its exceptional filmmaking and artistry, Studio Ghibli has earned both critical and popular praise through its fantastic stories of magic, adventure, friendship, and family.

In 1996, The Walt Disney Company partnered with Tokuma Shoten, granting Disney international distribution rights. Notable films include Princess Mononoke (1997) and Spirited Away (2002), the latter earning the Golden Bear and an Academy Award.

Food plays a symbolic and emotional role in Studio Ghibli films. Whether it's a comforting red bean bao in Spirited Away or soup in Ponyo, the culinary scenes deepen character connections and bring the magic to life. This cookbook is here to help you bring those dishes from the screen into your kitchen.



How to Use This Cookbook

The cookbook is divided into categories: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snacks and Street Food, and Desserts and Bakery Treats. While most dishes are Japanese, some are inspired by international cuisine shown in the films. Ingredient substitutions are mentioned either in the Special Ingredients and Equipment section or as tips under each recipe. Like animation, cooking is art-feel free to add your own twist!



Fun Facts about Studio Ghibli

  • Airplanes are common because Hayao Miyazaki's father was director of Miyazaki Airplanes.
  • "Ghibli" is an Italian word meaning "hot desert wind" and also the name of a WWII aircraft.
  • Spirited Away is the highest-grossing Japanese film and the only non-English film to win the Oscar for Best Animated Feature.
  • Miyazaki hand-drew the water in Ponyo.
  • Earwig and the Witch was the studio's first full CGI film, directed by Goro Miyazaki.



Special Ingredients and Equipment

Essential Utensils

  • Bamboo Sushi Mat - For rolling sushi tightly.
  • Rice Paddle - To fluff and serve rice.
  • Skewers - For grilling meats or veggies.
  • Lacquered Bowl - Fancy bowl often used for soups.
  • Mini Sauce Dishes - For soy sauce, chili sauce, etc.

Essential Ingredients

  • Bonito Flakes - Used in dashi broth.
  • Daikon - White radish for stews and frying.
  • Dashi Stock - Japanese soup base made from bonito and kelp.
  • Dried Kelp / Seaweed - Used in soups and salads (kombu, wakame).
  • Mirin, Miso, Soy Sauce - Common flavor bases.
  • Tofu - Available in silken, soft, or firm textures.
  • Togarashi - A Japanese 7-spice blend.
  • Umeboshi - Pickled plum with a unique flavor.
